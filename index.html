Story

I started my career designing interfaces for banking and logistics software — complex, high-stakes products where a confusing UI has real consequences. That early experience taught me to take user cognition seriously, and to see dense workflows not as unfortunate requirements but as design problems worth solving.

Over the years I moved into leadership: managing design teams, establishing critique cultures, and building the systems infrastructure (design tokens, shared libraries, governance models) that let organisations ship consistently at scale.

Today I work as a Principal UX Designer, owning design strategy across multiple product lines. I'm most energised by problems that sit at the edge of what's technically feasible and what users actually need — and by the cross-functional work required to close that gap.

Principles
01

Clarity over cleverness

The best interface is the one users don't notice. I prioritise immediate comprehension over visual ingenuity.

02

Research is not optional

Every assumption is a risk. I invest in understanding users before committing to solutions — even under tight timelines.

03

Systems enable scale

Good component work pays compound interest. I design systems that make the right thing easy and the wrong thing hard.

04

Design is a team sport

The best outcomes happen when engineers, PMs, and designers think together from the start — not in handoffs.
